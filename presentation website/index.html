You will need the following ingredients:

For the dough:

  • 500g wheat flour
  • 300ml water
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 5g fresh yeast

For the sauce:

  • 1 can pizza tomatoes
  • salt and pepper
  • a little bit sugar
  • basil
  • oregano

Preperation

Step 1:
After countless field tests, extensive Google research, and restaurant spying,
I have finally developed a recipe that tastes absolutely authentic and delicious—just like in Bella Italia!
Step2
First, sift the flour with the salt and pour it into a dough mixer or bread maker
with a dough function. Now dissolve the yeast in the lukewarm water and then add it to the flour and salt mixture (note from the
Chefkoch.de team recipe editing: The amount of yeast specified is correct and it is fresh yeast).
Step 3:
Then knead the ingredients for 20 (!) minutes to form an elastic, homogeneous dough.
Note: An electric mixer is almost essential here, but a hand mixer with dough hooks will do in a pinch.
Please do not attempt to knead by hand, as the dough will not achieve the desired softness and consistency.
Step 4:
After kneading, cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for two hours.
Then divide it into four 200 g pieces, shape them into balls, cover them again, and let them rise for another six hours.
Step 5:
So what is so special about this dough recipe? The secret lies not in exquisite
ingredients such as expensive pizza flour or high-quality olive oil, nor in superfluous ingredients such as beer, sugar, or eggs.
Rather, the smooth texture is achieved through long machine kneading! The extremely small amount of yeast,
together with the long fermentation, ensures an extremely aromatic dough.
Step 6:
Finally, a few tips for further processing: Roll out or stretch the dough as thinly
as possible! Anything less than 4 mm is ideal.
Step 7:
For the tomato sauce, simply puree a can of peeled tomatoes with (lots of) garlic, salt,
pepper, a little sugar, basil, and oregano while cold; do not cook. Then spread it in the center of the pizza and spread it
over the entire pizza in circular motions with a ladle. Don't use too much! A good pizza should not be
drowned in tomato sauce. If some areas of the dough don't get any sauce, it doesn't matter.
It's better to have a little less than a little more.
Step 8:
Preheat the oven to the highest setting! Mine reaches just under 260 °C, and the result
was great. Place the dough on the hot baking sheet (it's easier with baking paper) and
after just 3-4 minutes, the pizza is ready to enjoy.
